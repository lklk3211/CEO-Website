INDIAN CEO'S HEADING THE GLOBAL COMPANIES

1)Sundar Pichai, CEO of Google

Full name – Pichai Sundararajan

Born – 12th July, 1972

Place of Birth – Madurai, Tamil Nadu, India

Currently living : San Francisco Bay Area

From growing up in a two room apartment in Ashok Nagar, Chennai to being announced the CEO of Google LLC on 10 August, 2015, Sundar Pichai has come a long way. The soft-spoken 46 year old did his B. Tech in Metallurgical Engineering from IIT Kharagpur and then won a scholarship to study on material science and on semiconductors in physics from Stanford University, where he eventually earned his M.S. After that he completed his MBA from the Wharton School of University of Pennysylvania and worked as a management consultant at McKinsey & Company. He joined Google in the year 2004 and led the product management and innovation efforts for key products including Google Chrome OS and Google Drive.

2)Satya Nadella, CEO of Microsoft

Full name – Satya Narayana Nadella

Born – 19th August 1967

Place of Birth – Hyderabad, Telangana, India

Nadella, now 51 and a father of three kids, was named the head of Microsoft in February 2014. He has given 22 years of his life to the company and previously held the position of Executive Vice President of Microsoft’s Cloud and Enterprise Group before being handed over the position of CEO.The Hyderabad born holds a degree in electrical engineering from the Manipal Institute of Technology. After that he did his Masters in Computer Science from the University of Wisconsin, Milwaukee and MBA from the University of Chicago Booth School of Business.He revised Microsoft’s mission statement to “empower every person and every organization on the planet to achieve more” from it’s earlier mission of “a PC on every desk and in every home, running Microsoft software.”
“I fundamentally believe that if you are not learning new things, you stop doing great and useful things”, says Nadella on his unending desire for learning and interest in reading books.

3)Indra Nooyi, CEO of Pepsico(stepped Down in 2018)

Full name – Indra Krishnamurthy (born), after marriage- Indra Nooyi

Born –28 October, 1955

Place of Birth – Madras (now Chennai), Tamil Nadu, India

An inspiration for many, Indra Nooyi, is an Indian American business executive, currently serving as the Chairman of the second largest food and beverage business in the world, Pepsico. Born in a middle class Tamil speaking Indian family, Nooyi received her Bachelors degree in Physics, Chemistry and Mathematics from the University of Madras.Then she went on to achieve a PG Diploma from IIM Calcutta, and in 1978, was admitted to the Yale School of Management where she earned a Master’s degree in Public and Private Management. Coming from humble background, the young Nooyi worked part time jobs to support herself while college.She began her career with the position of product manager at Johnson and Johnson and then moved on to Pepsico. Today , she is ranked among the World’s 100 most powerful women and was ranked number 13 on the same. She is to be the first independent female director of the ICC Board.

4)Anshu Jain,President of Cantor Fitzgerald & former CO CEO of Deutsche Bank

Full name – Anshuman Jain

Born –7th January 1963(age 55)

Place of Birth – Jaipur, Rajasthan, India

Anshuman Jain is a British Indian business executive and is currently serving as the President of Cantor Fitzgerald. He was a member of the Deutsche Bank’s Management Board and remained a consultant to the bank until January 2016.He holds a degree in Economics from Shri Ram College of Commerce, DU and an MBA in Finance from the University of Massachusetts Amherst. After having completed his education, he started working as an analyst in derivatives research at Kidder, Peabody and Co. where he worked from 1985 to 1988. He joined Deutsche Bank’s nascent markets business in 1995.An ardent conservationist, Anshu is currently working with environment and wildlife conservation groups in many parts of the world. He is also a trustee of Chance to Shine, a leading UK based sports charity whose mission is to spread the power of cricket throughout schools and communities.

5)Shantanu Narayen, CEO of Adobe Systems and President of the board of Adobe foundation

Full name – Shantanu Narayen

Born – 27th May, 1962(age 56)

Place of Birth – Hyderabad, Telangana, India

Shantanu Narayen, an Indian American business executive joined Adobe in the year 1998 as a senior vice-president of worldwide product research and then was promoted to the position of executive Vice President and finally appointed to his current position in the November 2007 at the age of 45.He was born in a Telugu speaking family with his mother being a teacher of American Literature and his father running a plastics company in Hyderabad. 6) Ivan Menezes, CEO of Diageo

Full name – Ivan Manuel Menezes

Born – July 1959(age 59)

Place of Birth – Pune, India

Mr. Menezes, is an Indian born American/British business executive. He began his career with Nestle in 1981 and joined Diageo in the year 1997. After taking up the position of CEO, one of his biggest moves was buying a majority stake in India’s United Spirits company. The company is known to own several other brands as well, namely, Guinness, Johnnie Walker Whisky, Smirnoff vodka and Captain Morgan rum.He was educated at St. Stephen's College, Delhi and Indian Institute of Management Ahmedabad, and the Northwestern University's Kellogg School of Management. He has been the chief executive officer (CEO) of Diageo, a FTSE 100 British multinational alcoholic beverages company, since 1 July 2013, succeeding Paul S. Walsh.

7) Sanjay Jha, Former CEO of Global Foundries

Full name – Sanjay Kumar Jha

Born – 1963

Place of Birth – Sultanganj, Bihar, India

Sanjay Kumar Jha holds a Bachelor of Science from the University of Liverpool and a PhD in Electronics Engineering from the University of Strathclyde. 8)Ajaypal Singh Banga, Executive Chairman of MasterCard

Full name – Ajaypal Singh Banga

Born – 1960

Place of Birth – Pune, India

An Indian Sikh American business executive, Ajaypal Singh Banga , is a Padma Shri Award recipient, and has been serving as the CEO and president of MasterCard since April, 2010. In the year 2015, President Barack Obama appointed him to serve as a Member of the President’s Advisory Committee for Trade Policy and Negotiations. Born to a decorated army general of the Indian Army, Banga spent his entire childhood and teenage years shifting across different cities in the country and moving from one school to other. He graduated from St. Stephens College, Delhi University with BA in Economics and holds an MBA from the Indian Institute of Management, Ahmedabad.Banga is the former chairman of the U.S.-India Business Council (USIBC) representing more than 300 of the largest international companies investing in India, and chairman of the International Chamber of Commerce.

12)Rakesh Kapoor, Former CEO of Reckitt Benckiser

Full name – Rakesh Kapoor

Born – 4th August 1958

Place of Birth – Bareilly, India

Rakesh Kapoor (born 4 August 1958) is an Indian businessman. 16) Arvind Krishna , CEO of IBM

Full name –Arvind Krishna

Born – 1962

Arvind Krishna is the Chairman and Chief Executive Officer of IBM. 17) Jayshree Ullal - CEO, Arista Networks

Full name –Jayshree V. Ullal

Born –March 27, 1961

Place of Birth –London, England

Jayshree V. Ullal (born March 27, 1961) is an American billionaire businesswoman, president and CEO of Arista Networks, a cloud networking company responsible for the deployment of 10/25/40/50/100 Gigabit Ethernet networking in the data center.Ullal was born in London, and raised in New Delhi, India, through her school years. She eventually attended San Francisco State University where she graduated with a B.S. in engineering (electrical). She went on to Santa Clara University where she received a master's degree in engineering management.Ullal began her career with engineering and strategy positions at Advanced Micro Devices (AMD) and Fairchild Semiconductor. She was director of internetworking products at Ungermann-Bass for four years before joining Crescendo Communications. At Crescendo, Ullal became vice president of marketing, working with 100-Mbit/s over copper, the first CDDI products and first generation Ethernet switching.
